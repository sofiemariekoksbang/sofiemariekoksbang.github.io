I am group leader of the Inhomogeneous Cosmology group at The University of Southern Denmark. Our group codex is presented below.

1. Purpose:

Our research group is committed to maintaining a respectful, inclusive, and intellectually rigorous environment where everyone can contribute fully. This code of conduct exists to support productive scientific discussion, mutual respect, and a positive working atmosphere for all members of the group.

2. Core values:

We value
 - Respect for people, ideas, and differences
 - Open scientific debate conducted professionally
 - Curiosity, humility, and willingness to learn
 - Shared responsibility for the group atmosphere

3. Expected behaviors

All group members are expected to:
 - Treat colleagues with respect, regardless of background, seniority, nationality, culture, or identity
 - Critique ideas, results, and methods rather than individuals
 - Allow others to finish speaking and make space for all voices in discussions
 - Disagree professionally and constructively
 - Be mindful of language and generalizations when discussing countries, cultures, or groups
 - Assume good intentions while remaining open to feedback about the impact of one’s words or behavior


4. Behaviors to avoid

The following behaviors can undermine a healthy research environment and should be avoided:
 - Interrupting or talking over others
 - Condescending, dismissive, or sarcastic language
 - Broad negative generalizations about countries, cultures, or groups
 - Presenting opinions as facts outside one’s area of expertise
 - Personal attacks or comments directed at individuals rather than ideas
 - Creating an atmosphere where others feel hesitant to speak or contribute


5. Self-reflection

We encourage everyone --- including the group leader --- to regularly reflect on their own behavior and ask questions such as:
 - What did I do today to help create a positive and productive atmosphere?
 - Did I listen actively and allow others to finish speaking?
 - Did I disagree respectfully and focus on ideas rather than people?
 - Did I make assumptions or generalizations about someone’s background, culture, or country?
 - If someone felt hurt or uncomfortable because of something I said, would I be open to hearing that?

6. Addressing concerns

If issues arise, they should ideally be addressed early and respectfully. Group members are encouraged to speak directly with one another when appropriate. The group leader is also available to facilitate conversations or to address concerns confidentially. The goal is always learning, improvement, and maintaining a constructive research environment, rather than assigning blame.


This codex is intended as a living codex and may be revised based on feedback and experience.
