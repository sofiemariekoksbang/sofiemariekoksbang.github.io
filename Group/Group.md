<h2>Group Codex</h2>

<p>
I lead the Inhomogeneous Cosmology group at the University of Southern Denmark. 
Our group codex is presented below.
</p>

<hr>

<h3>1. Purpose</h3>
<p>
Our research group is committed to maintaining a respectful, inclusive, and intellectually rigorous environment where everyone can contribute fully. Our group codex exists to support productive scientific discussion, mutual respect, and a positive working atmosphere for all members of the group.
</p>

<h3>2. Core Values</h3>
<ul>
  <li>Respect for people, ideas, and differences</li>
  <li>Open scientific debate conducted professionally</li>
  <li>Curiosity, humility, and willingness to learn</li>
  <li>Shared responsibility for the group atmosphere</li>
</ul>

<h3>3. Expected Behaviors</h3>
<p>All group members are expected to:</p>
<ul>
  <li>Treat colleagues with respect, regardless of background, seniority, nationality, culture, or identity</li>
  <li>Critique ideas, results, and methods rather than individuals</li>
  <li>Allow others to finish speaking and make space for all voices in discussions</li>
  <li>Disagree professionally and constructively</li>
  <li>Be mindful of language and generalizations when discussing countries, cultures, or groups</li>
  <li>Assume good intentions while remaining open to feedback about the impact of one’s words or behavior</li>
</ul>

<h3>4. Behaviors to Avoid</h3>
<p>The following behaviors can undermine a healthy research environment and should be avoided:</p>
<ul>
  <li>Interrupting or talking over others</li>
  <li>Condescending, dismissive, or sarcastic language</li>
  <li>Broad negative generalizations about countries, cultures, or groups</li>
  <li>Presenting opinions as facts outside one’s area of expertise</li>
  <li>Personal attacks or comments directed at individuals rather than ideas</li>
  <li>Creating an atmosphere where others feel hesitant to speak or contribute</li>
</ul>

<h3>5. Self-Reflection</h3>
<p>
We encourage everyone — including the group leader — to regularly reflect on their own behavior and ask:
</p>
<ul>
  <li>What did I do today to help create a positive and productive atmosphere?</li>
  <li>Did I listen actively and allow others to finish speaking?</li>
  <li>Did I disagree respectfully and focus on ideas rather than people?</li>
  <li>Did I make assumptions or generalizations about someone’s background, culture, or country?</li>
  <li>If someone felt hurt or uncomfortable because of something I said, would I be open to hearing that?</li>
</ul>

<h3>6. Addressing Concerns</h3>
<p>
If issues arise, they should ideally be addressed early and respectfully.
</p>
<ul>
  <li>Group members are encouraged to speak directly with one another when appropriate</li>
  <li>The group leader is available to facilitate conversations or address concerns confidentially</li>
</ul>
<p>
The goal is always learning, improvement, and maintaining a constructive research environment — not assigning blame.
</p>

<hr>

<h3>Living Document</h3>
<p>
This codex is intended as a living document and may be revised based on feedback and experience.
</p>
